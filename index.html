ADA LOVELACE

1815-1852

"That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show."

Biography

Ada Lovelace (1815-1852) was a pioneering English mathematician and writer, widely recognized as the first computer programmer. She is best known for her work on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine, which included an algorithm for calculating Bernoulli numbers. Her work on the Analytical Engine was based on her understanding of its potential to go beyond calculation and perform tasks such as composing music and generating graphics. She died at the age of 36 of cancer in 1852, but her legacy has continued to inspire generations of scientists and mathematicians. Ada Lovelace Day is celebrated annually to honor her contributions.

MADAM C.J WALKER

1867-1919

"I had to make my own living and my own opportunity. But I made it! Don't sit down and wait for the opportunities to come. Get up and make them."

Biography

Madam C.J. Walker (born Sarah Breedlove) was an African American entrepreneur and philanthropist who is known for becoming one of the first self-made female millionaires in the United States. She was born on December 23, 1867, in Delta, Louisiana, to parents who were recently freed slaves. After experiencing hair loss, Madam C.J. Walker developed her own hair-care products for African American women. She began selling her products door-to-door and eventually established the Madam C.J. Walker Manufacturing Company, which became very successful. She used her wealth to support various charitable causes, including the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) and the National Conference on Lynching. Madam C.J. Walker is remembered for her entrepreneurship and philanthropy, as well as her role in promoting economic empowerment and self-reliance for African American women. Her life story has been the subject of many books and documentaries, and in 2020, a Netflix limited series called "Self Made" was released, which depicts her life and achievements. Madam C.J. Walker died on May 25, 1919, at the age of 51. The cause of her death was kidney failure and complications from hypertension. She was buried at Woodlawn Cemetery in The Bronx, New York City. Despite her relatively short life, Madam C.J. Walker left a lasting legacy as a successful entrepreneur and philanthropist who worked to empower and uplift African American women.

StEVE JOBS

1955-2011

"The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."

Biography

Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was an American entrepreneur, inventor, and innovator best known as the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the technology industry and a pioneer of the modern personal computer and mobile device era. He was born in San Francisco, California, and was adopted shortly after birth. Jobs attended Reed College in Portland, Oregon, but dropped out after six months and returned to California, where he became involved in the computer industry. He co-founded Apple Computer with his friend Steve Wozniak in 1976 and helped to establish the personal computer industry. Jobs was forced out of Apple in the 1980s, but returned in 1997 and led its turnaround. He was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in 2003 and passed away on October 5, 2011, at the age of 56.
